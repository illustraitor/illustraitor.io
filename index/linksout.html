Links Out

-------------------

Why did I do this?

I read this article by Melon King that talked about the importance of linking outside your website, and I was convinced! Plus, I found most of the useful information in my art journey from other folks on the internet. I think it's a good idea to pass on knowledge and resources where you can. Below are links to various artists and resources that I have come across in my time browsing the internet that I will update periodically (there's so much more I want to share, compiling is just time-consuming!).

I hope you find something new and interesting!

 

General Artists:

Some you may already have heard of! These are a collection of artists who's work I've stumbled across over the years that caught my eye.

  • Celine Kim: A visual development artist with a stunning collection of work. I really dig the Phantom of the Opera vis dev she made.
  • Kat Tsai: Their illustrations feel like fleeting memories, her control over color is impeccable!
  • Celia Lowenthal: I've been a fan of her work for a while, she has a bold pallet and heavy inks which is always a weakness for me.
  • Kaylee Rowena: Not only are their illustrations eerie and whimsical, I was inspired by them to make this website!

 

Special Treatment:

This is the section for some of my friends and acquaintances! 

 

  • Amy Nguyen: Amy is a vis dev and storyboard artist with a whimsical and energetic style. Annnd she's one of the funniest people I know!
  • Janet Doan: Janet's style is so cute, and she does a whole lot of different illustration-related mediums. She primarily works with watercolor (neat!) but can do anything.
  • Anna Dinh: Is also a vis dev artist and storyboarder! She's is talented in so many creative mediums and smart as hell.
  • Genisis Lugo: They make killer storyboards and have a tendency of falling into small and niche fandoms and being their bread and butter for fanart, which I think metal.
  • Sarah Putros: A teacher I had back in college who was (and still is) a fountain of knowledge, her students are lucky to have her.
  • Jenni:  A joy to be around, they are full of love for their community and the earth. Jenni's painting's at times feel like they are breathing, others feel like a memory. Hard to explain, but I love it.

 

Illustration Resources:

My go-to sites

  • Crouqis-Cafe:  A paid alternative to Line of Action for figure drawing reference. Line of action is great, I just like Crouqis' selection a little more.
  • William Nguyen's Head Models: Free head reference
  • Healthline Anatomical models: This is for medical students but it's so cool! You can peel back layers of the body and see where the muscles and bones connect with one another.

 

 

Website Building:

This list is copied from my About This Website page

  • 32-Bit Cafe | Resource List: A huge list of website making resources, a bit overwhelming to look through but just pick what sounds useful to you.
  • 32-Bit Cafe | Creating Your Own Website: Good getting started info.
  • Organization Recommendation: I got this from the 32-Bit Cafe masterlist, but it would have saved me pain if I had read it early on in my process.
  • A Complete Guide to Flexbox: My layout heavily uses the flexbox element, this is a good summary of its components.
  • W3 Schools: This site has general info on pretty much anything code related. It's one of those sites that's so popular I thought it wasn't worth mentioning, but here it is just in case.
  • Stackoverflow: This was my oracle. I never posted any questions myself (I'm shy~) but it almost always had answers.
  • Responsinator: Check what your website looks like on other devices!
  • HTML Editor: I use this to type out my text before putting in my html doc. My browser has a spellcheck and the editor formats everything for you. Handy!
  • Rarebit Webcomic Layout: Handy if you have a comic you wan to display
  • smolcss: I wish I had found this sooner! It's CSS codes for responsive elemnts.
