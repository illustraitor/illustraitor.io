Why did I do this?

 

While very clean and seamless most modern website layouts look, I had a really hard time finding one that fit my aesthetic. I had also been falling into neocities rabbit holes and looking through a variety of websites with fun layouts. Some of my favorites are here. That being said, the site we are currently on is pretty basic and probably doesn't stray too far from your typical squarespace portfolio. But, having a responsive portfolio makes sense since you'll be showing recruiters or showing folks your website on your phone. It's just business, I just added flair where I could without sacrificing too much responsivesness1. I hope to add even more flair to it as time goes on. While nowhere near as cool, I took a lot of inspo from Kaylee Rowena's website. If you look at their illustration gallery, you'll see the elements perfectly collapse on top of one another when re-sizing. Also, they have such a stellar collection of artwork.

1. Responsiveness is the ability for a website to adapt to various screen sizes and browsers.  

 

Want to make your own website?

 

Here are a list of resources I found the most helpful, in no particular order. Gotta be honest though, most of what I did was peek under the hood of other folks' websites (right click + inspect ) and then try things I liked for myself; and then praying someone on stackoverflow had already asked about a hyper-specific problem I was having. If I could start over though, I would peek through these resources before even starting. A lot of the issues I was having would have been solved if I had justâ€¦read ahead. I like to do trial by fire, but this was a lot of fire. Hell, even. Also, I recomend making a mock-up in an image editor before getting started, for reference and motivation. 

 

  • 32-Bit Cafe | Resource List: A huge list of website making resources, a bit overwhelming to look through but just pick what sounds useful to you.
  • 32-Bit Cafe | Creating Your Own Website: Good getting started info.
  • Organization Recommendation: I got this from the 32-Bit Cafe masterlist, but it would have saved me pain if I had read it early on in my process.
  • A Complete Guide to Flexbox: My layout heavily uses the flexbox element, this is a good summary of its components.
  • W3 Schools: This site has general info on pretty much anything code related. It's one of those sites that's so popular I thought it wasn't worth mentioning, but here it is just in case.
  • Stackoverflow: This was my oracle. I never posted any questions myself (I'm shy~) but it almost always had answers.
  • Responsinator: Check what your website looks like on other devices!
  • HTML Editor: I use this to type out my text before putting in my html doc. My browser has a spellcheck and the editor formats everything for you. Handy!
  • Rarebit Webcomic Layout: Handy if you have a comic you wan to display
  • smolcss: I wish I had found this sooner! It's CSS codes for responsive elemnts.
